1) What is colorectal cancer prediction?
Colorectal cancer prediction refers to the process of using a predictive model to assess the likelihood of an individual having colorectal cancer based on certain input data, such as gene expression datasets.
2) How accurate is the colorectal cancer prediction on your website?
The accuracy of the colorectal cancer prediction on our website depends on the performance of the underlying predictive model. Our model has been trained on a large dataset and has shown promising results in previous evaluations. However, it's important to note that no prediction model is 100% accurate, and individual results may vary.
3) What input data is required for cancer prediction?
To perform cancer prediction on our website, you need to provide a gene expression dataset. This dataset should contain relevant information about gene expressions. The first row should be the headers and the first column should be the ID of the specimen. You will specify the target column name, which represents the class label indicating the presence or absence of cancer. Lastly, you can indicate the number of columns to be selected for the feature selection process.
4) Can I use my own dataset for colorectal cancer prediction?
Absolutely! Our website allows you to train a new model using your own dataset. This feature enables customization and adaptation of the predictive model to your specific requirements.
5) How can I assess the performance of the predictive model?
Once you submit the data and the backend code finishes executing, you will be presented with performance metrics of the model. These metrics include measures like accuracy, sensitivity, specificity, and AUC, which provide insights into the model's performance in predicting colorectal cancer.
6) Can I download the preprocessed dataset used during model training?
Yes, you can! We promote transparency and reproducibility by allowing users to download the preprocessed dataset used during the model training process. This enables you to review the data and understand how the model was trained.
7) What can I do with the predicted results?
Once the prediction is generated, you have several options. You can download the predicted class labels for your dataset, which can be useful for further analysi. You can also choose to predict new data again or even decide to train a new model based on your specific requirements.
8) Is my data secure and private?
We take data security and privacy seriously. All data you provide is handled with strict confidentiality and is used solely for the purpose of generating predictions. We do not share your data with any third parties.
