About Us

Precancer is a tool for predicting the probability of having cancer by the use of gene expressions of an individual. Cancer is one of the leading causes of death to humans, which contributes around 10 million fatalities each year. In 2020, there were 29530 citizens in Malaysia who died due to cancer. Moreover, more than half of cancer patients are diagnosed late and end up in Stage 3 or 4 when it is detected. As a result, the probability of surviving and having a successful treatment is one in a million. As a consequence, the development of molecular biomarker lab tests provides a viable alternative for a rapid, non-invasive, and accurate early cancer detection.
